What Are Reflexes?

Primitive reflexes are automatic movements that should integrate by age 1. When retained, they cause developmental delays.

The 7 Major Primitive Reflexes

Moro Reflex - The Startle Response ▼

What it is: The infant startle response that should integrate by 4-6 months.

Signs if retained:

  • Anxiety and emotional outbursts
  • Sensitivity to light, sound, touch
  • Motion sickness
  • Poor impulse control
  • Difficulty with transitions

Integration exercises: Deep pressure, rhythmic rocking, starfish exercise, breathing techniques

ATNR - Asymmetrical Tonic Neck Reflex ▼

What it is: When head turns, same-side arm extends, opposite arm bends.

Signs if retained:

  • Poor handwriting and fine motor skills
  • Difficulty crossing midline
  • Reading and tracking problems
  • Poor hand-eye coordination
  • Balance issues

Integration exercises: Cross-crawl, lazy 8s, midline crossing activities

STNR - Symmetrical Tonic Neck Reflex ▼

What it is: Head position affects arm and leg tone when on hands and knees.

Signs if retained:

  • Poor posture (slumping at desk)
  • Messy eating habits
  • Difficulty sitting still
  • W-sitting position
  • Clumsiness

Integration exercises: Cat-cow stretches, crawling games, wall push-ups

Spinal Galant - Back Sensitivity Reflex ▼

What it is: Stroking the lower back causes hip rotation to same side.

Signs if retained:

  • Bedwetting past age 5
  • Fidgeting and hyperactivity
  • Attention difficulties
  • Sensitivity to clothing tags/waistbands
  • Poor concentration when sitting

Integration exercises: Snow angels, back brushing, swimming movements

TLR - Tonic Labyrinthine Reflex ▼

What it is: Head position affects muscle tone throughout body.

Signs if retained:

  • Poor balance and coordination
  • Motion sickness
  • Difficulty judging space and distance
  • Poor muscle tone
  • Difficulty with sequencing

Integration exercises: Rolling activities, balance board, vestibular activities

Palmar & Plantar - Grasp Reflexes ▼

What it is: Automatic grasping when palm or sole is touched.

Signs if retained:

  • Poor pencil grip
  • Messy handwriting
  • Speech articulation issues
  • Toe walking
  • Difficulty with fine motor tasks

Integration exercises: Hand exercises, barefoot activities, finger games

Rooting & Suck - Oral Reflexes ▼

What it is: Infant feeding reflexes for survival.

Signs if retained:

  • Speech delays and articulation problems
  • Picky eating or texture issues
  • Drooling past appropriate age
  • Thumb sucking past age 5
  • Difficulty with oral motor control

Integration exercises: Oral motor exercises, blowing activities, chewing exercises

Top 6 Essential Exercises

🐱 Cat-Cow Stretch

Time: 60-90 seconds

Benefits: Integrates STNR, improves posture, builds core strength

On hands and knees, alternate between arching and rounding your back slowly.

🚶 Cross-Crawl

Time: 60-90 seconds

Benefits: Integrates ATNR, improves coordination, enhances learning

Touch opposite elbow to knee while marching in place rhythmically.

⭐ Starfish

Time: 5 repetitions

Benefits: Integrates Moro, reduces anxiety, improves emotional regulation

Lie on back, spread limbs wide, then curl into ball. Repeat slowly.

🏠 Wall Push-Ups

Time: 10 repetitions

Benefits: Proprioceptive input, calms nervous system, builds strength

Stand arm's length from wall, lean in and push back slowly.

♾️ Lazy 8s

Time: 2 minutes

Benefits: Visual tracking, reading preparation, calms mind

Trace large figure-8 pattern sideways with finger, following with eyes.

🌊 Rocking

Time: 2-3 minutes

Benefits: Vestibular input, emotional regulation, sleep preparation

Gentle rhythmic rocking in chair or on floor, forward and back.
